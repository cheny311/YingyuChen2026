1 — Concept

Perhaps it functions like a pause: a brief moment to catch one’s breath. In later iterations, this misaligned form of communication was further systematized. HaHaHa evolved into an interactive object that produces prerecorded laughter through bodily interaction. Participants can generate laughter without emotional investment, as if happiness itself can be captured, automated, and projected. By separating laughter from emotion, the work presents laughter as a mechanical response. Through the deconstruction of the Chinese character “哈,” the project articulates my understanding of social discipline as something embedded in everyday gestures. .
