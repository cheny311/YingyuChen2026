1 — Concept

In many situations People ask questions like “How are you?” or “How’s your day?” These questions sound caring, yet they are not meant to invite honest answers. The responses are often already assumed: Good. Fine. Not bad. As an outsider, I often find myself confused: if we do not genuinely care about each other’s inner states, why do we keep asking? In many conversational settings, language appears to flow continuously, yet it does not truly lead to understanding. Familiar phrases are repeated, expected responses are delivered, and communication gradually becomes something performed rather than sincerely expressed.

In this project, I collected high-frequency social phrases and transformed them into a roll of “cheat notes.” Participants effortlessly pull out random sentences and use them as replies. Language no longer needs to be thought through—it simply needs to be retrieved.
